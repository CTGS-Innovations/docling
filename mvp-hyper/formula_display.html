Diffusion Probabilistic Models - Mathematical Formulas

From "Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Models" by Ho, Jain & Abbeel (2020)
Diffusion Process Chain
$x_T \leftarrow \cdots \leftarrow x_t \leftarrow x_{t-1} \leftarrow \cdots \leftarrow x_0$
The forward diffusion process that gradually adds noise to data $x_0$ until it becomes pure noise $x_T$.
Reverse Process Distribution
$p_\theta(x_{t-1}|x_t)$
The learned reverse process that denoises from timestep $t$ to $t-1$. This is parameterized by neural network parameters $\theta$.
Forward Process Distribution
$q(x_t|x_{t-1})$
The fixed forward process that adds Gaussian noise from timestep $t-1$ to $t$.
Complete Diffusion Framework
$$\begin{align} \text{Forward process: } &q(x_{1:T}|x_0) = \prod_{t=1}^T q(x_t|x_{t-1}) \\ \text{Reverse process: } &p_\theta(x_{0:T}) = p(x_T) \prod_{t=1}^T p_\theta(x_{t-1}|x_t) \\ \text{Training objective: } &\mathbb{E}_{q(x_{1:T}|x_0)} \left[ \log \frac{q(x_{1:T}|x_0)}{p_\theta(x_{0:T})} \right] \end{align}$$
The complete mathematical framework showing the forward process $q$, reverse process $p_\theta$, and variational training objective.
Key Properties
$$\begin{align} q(x_t|x_0) &= \mathcal{N}(x_t; \sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_t} x_0, (1-\bar{\alpha}_t) I) \\ p_\theta(x_{t-1}|x_t) &= \mathcal{N}(x_{t-1}; \mu_\theta(x_t, t), \Sigma_\theta(x_t, t)) \\ \bar{\alpha}_t &= \prod_{s=1}^t \alpha_s, \quad \alpha_t = 1 - \beta_t \end{align}$$
Direct sampling from any timestep, parameterization of the reverse process, and the noise schedule relationships.
